# Expected Significance And Impact

## Main Significance

This note is designed as a theory-method diagnostic paper, not as an
observational discovery claim. Its main contribution is the disciplined
diagnostic framework around a bounded effective operator:

```text
W(x) = 1 + rho*x^3,  rho <= 4
```

The operator is bounded before comparison with reconstructed cosmological
diagnostics, which makes it stronger than a post-hoc amplitude fit. The cubic
formula is not the whole contribution; the more durable contribution is the
anti-overfitting protocol around it.

## Why It May Matter

- It gives a compact way to discuss finite projection-memory effects without
  requiring the reader to accept the full Tau Core program.
- It interfaces naturally with current FLRW consistency, Dyer-Roeder, and
  cosmic-backreaction discussions.
- It shows how to handle sign-unstable reconstructed diagnostics without
  silently overclaiming.
- It creates a bridge from speculative projection theory to reproducible
  cosmological stress tests.
- It gives a concrete example of reconstruction-sensitive sign handling.

## Expected Audience

The likely first audience is narrow but relevant:

```text
cosmological consistency-test researchers
backreaction / light-propagation specialists
modified-gravity phenomenology readers
methods-focused theory readers
```

## Expected Impact Level

```text
near-term: niche theory-method interest

high-impact route: full covariance comparison with public likelihood products
```

## What Would Increase The Impact

- Full covariance or shrinkage-covariance likelihood comparison.
- Direct use of published likelihood/data products rather than reconstructed
  diagnostic envelopes.
- A standalone reproduction pipeline independent of the private research tree.
- A sharper relation to known backreaction and Dyer-Roeder observables.

## Claim Boundary

The paper should not be framed as evidence that Tau Core is correct. It should
be framed as a reproducible finite-memory projection diagnostic that may become
important if it continues to survive stronger likelihood tests.
